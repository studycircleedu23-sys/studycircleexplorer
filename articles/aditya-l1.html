☀️ ISRO Aditya-L1 Mission

Explore India's first solar observatory designed to study the Sun, solar activity, and space weather from the L1 Lagrange Point.

30 June 2026 5 min read Study Circle Explorer

What is Aditya-L1?

Aditya-L1 is ISRO's first dedicated mission to study the Sun. Instead of orbiting the Sun directly, the spacecraft operates from the Sun-Earth L1 Lagrange Point, approximately 1.5 million kilometers from Earth.

This special location allows Aditya-L1 to continuously observe the Sun without interruptions caused by eclipses or Earth's shadow.

⭐ Key Fact

Aditya-L1 is India's first space-based solar observatory and one of the few missions in the world dedicated to studying the Sun.

Mission Objectives

The mission studies the Sun's outer atmosphere (corona), solar flares, coronal mass ejections (CMEs), and the solar wind. Scientists use this information to better understand how solar activity affects Earth and nearby space.

Aditya-L1 also helps improve space weather forecasting, protecting satellites, astronauts, navigation systems, and communication networks.

Why the L1 Point?

The L1 Lagrange Point is a unique position where the gravitational pull of the Earth and the Sun balance each other. From this location, Aditya-L1 has an uninterrupted view of the Sun, allowing continuous scientific observations.

🚀 Mission Highlights

  • ✔ India's first solar observatory
  • ✔ Continuous observation of the Sun
  • ✔ Study solar flares and CMEs
  • ✔ Monitor space weather
  • ✔ Operates from the L1 Lagrange Point

Why Aditya-L1 Matters

Understanding the Sun is essential because solar storms can affect satellites, GPS systems, radio communications, and power grids on Earth. The mission provides valuable scientific data that helps researchers predict and understand these events.

Aditya-L1 also strengthens India's role in solar physics research and international space science.

• Studies the Sun from the L1 point.
