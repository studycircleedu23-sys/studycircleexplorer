🌌 Sagittarius A*

Meet the supermassive black hole hidden at the center of our Milky Way Galaxy.

16 July 2026 5 min read Study Circle Explorer

What is Sagittarius A*?

Sagittarius A* (pronounced "Sagittarius A-star") is the supermassive black hole located at the center of the Milky Way Galaxy. It contains approximately four million times the mass of our Sun and lies about 26,000 light-years from Earth.

Although it cannot be seen directly, astronomers have confirmed its existence by studying the motion of stars orbiting an invisible object with an enormous gravitational pull.

⭐ Quick Fact

Sagittarius A* was first imaged by the Event Horizon Telescope collaboration in 2022, providing humanity's first direct view of our galaxy's central black hole.

How Was It Discovered?

For decades, astronomers tracked stars moving around the center of the Milky Way. Their extremely fast orbits revealed the presence of an invisible object with immense mass concentrated into a tiny region.

This evidence confirmed that Sagittarius A* is a supermassive black hole.

How Big is Sagittarius A*?

Sagittarius A* has a mass of about 4 million Suns. Although its mass is enormous, the event horizon is only about 24 million kilometers across, making it much smaller than many other known supermassive black holes.

Compared to the giant black hole TON 618, Sagittarius A* is relatively small, but it still dominates the center of our galaxy through its immense gravitational influence.

🌌 Did You Know?

Our entire Solar System is orbiting Sagittarius A* along with billions of other stars. It takes the Sun about 225–250 million years to complete one orbit around the Milky Way.

Why is Sagittarius A* Important?

Studying Sagittarius A* helps scientists understand how galaxies form and evolve. Nearly every large galaxy appears to contain a supermassive black hole at its center, making Sagittarius A* an excellent laboratory for studying extreme gravity.

Observations from the Event Horizon Telescope, the James Webb Space Telescope, and other observatories continue to reveal new details about the environment surrounding this mysterious object.

Amazing Facts

  • ⭐ Located about 26,000 light-years from Earth.
  • ⭐ Contains roughly 4 million times the mass of the Sun.
  • ⭐ Lies at the exact center of the Milky Way.
  • ⭐ First directly imaged by the Event Horizon Telescope in 2022.
  • ⭐ The Sun and our Solar System orbit Sagittarius A* once every 225–250 million years.

Quick Summary

Sagittarius A* is the supermassive black hole at the center of the Milky Way Galaxy. Although invisible, its enormous gravity controls the motion of nearby stars and plays a key role in the evolution of our galaxy. It remains one of the most important objects studied by astronomers.
