🔥 Hawking Radiation

Discover Stephen Hawking's groundbreaking theory that suggests black holes slowly lose energy and may eventually disappear.

16 July 2026 6 min read Study Circle Explorer

What is Hawking Radiation?

Hawking Radiation is a theoretical process proposed by physicist Stephen Hawking in 1974. According to this theory, black holes are not completely black. Instead, they can slowly emit tiny amounts of energy due to quantum effects occurring near the event horizon.

Over extremely long periods of time, this process causes a black hole to lose mass. If no additional matter falls into it, the black hole could eventually shrink and disappear completely.

⭐ Key Discovery

Stephen Hawking combined ideas from quantum mechanics and Einstein's theory of gravity to predict that black holes can slowly evaporate.

How Does Hawking Radiation Work?

According to quantum physics, tiny particle-antiparticle pairs constantly appear and disappear throughout empty space. Near a black hole's event horizon, one particle may fall into the black hole while the other escapes into space.

To a distant observer, it appears as though the black hole has emitted a particle. This gradual loss of energy is what scientists call Hawking Radiation.

Can Black Holes Really Evaporate?

According to Hawking's theory, every black hole slowly loses energy by emitting Hawking Radiation. As energy escapes, the black hole's mass decreases over time. This process is incredibly slow for large black holes and may take far longer than the current age of the universe.

Small black holes would evaporate much faster than supermassive ones because they emit Hawking Radiation more rapidly.

🔥 Amazing Fact

A supermassive black hole may take around 10100 years or more to completely evaporate—far longer than the universe has existed so far.

Why is Hawking Radiation Important?

Hawking Radiation connects two of the biggest theories in physics: Einstein's General Relativity and Quantum Mechanics. It suggests that black holes are not completely permanent and has sparked major research into the nature of space, time, and information.

Scientists are still working to understand whether information falling into a black hole is truly lost or somehow preserved. This question is known as the Black Hole Information Paradox and remains one of the greatest unsolved problems in modern physics.

Amazing Facts

  • ⭐ Proposed by Stephen Hawking in 1974.
  • ⭐ Suggests black holes slowly lose mass over time.
  • ⭐ Has never been directly observed because the radiation is extremely weak.
  • ⭐ Plays an important role in modern theoretical physics.
  • ⭐ Led to decades of research on black holes and quantum gravity.

Quick Summary

Hawking Radiation is a theoretical prediction that black holes emit tiny amounts of energy due to quantum effects near the event horizon. Over immense periods of time, this process may cause black holes to slowly evaporate, making Hawking's idea one of the most revolutionary discoveries in modern astrophysics.
