🛰️ ISRO XPoSat Mission

Discover India's first X-ray Polarimeter Satellite and how it helps scientists study black holes, neutron stars, pulsars, and the most extreme objects in the universe.

30 June 2026 5 min read Study Circle Explorer

What is XPoSat?

XPoSat (X-ray Polarimeter Satellite) is India's first dedicated mission to study the polarization of X-rays emitted by some of the universe's most energetic objects, including black holes, neutron stars, pulsars, and active galaxies.

The mission helps astronomers understand how matter behaves under extreme gravity and magnetic fields.

⭐ Key Fact

XPoSat is India's first space observatory dedicated to X-ray polarization astronomy.

Mission Objectives

The primary objective of XPoSat is to measure the polarization of X-rays from bright cosmic sources. These measurements provide valuable information about the structure, magnetic fields, and physical processes occurring around compact celestial objects.

Scientists will use this data to better understand the behavior of matter in some of the most extreme environments known in the universe.

Scientific Instruments

The satellite carries advanced scientific instruments, including the POLIX (Polarimeter Instrument in X-rays) and XSPECT (X-ray Spectroscopy and Timing instrument), enabling detailed observations of energetic cosmic phenomena.

🚀 Mission Highlights

  • ✔ India's first X-ray polarimetry mission
  • ✔ Study black holes and neutron stars
  • ✔ Observe pulsars and active galaxies
  • ✔ Advanced X-ray astronomy instruments
  • ✔ Improve understanding of extreme space environments

Why XPoSat Matters

XPoSat strengthens India's capabilities in space astronomy and contributes valuable scientific data to the global astronomy community. The mission complements other space observatories by revealing information that cannot be obtained through ordinary imaging alone.

Its discoveries will help scientists better understand some of the universe's most powerful and mysterious objects.

🌌 Quick Summary

• India's first X-ray polarimetry satellite.
• Studies black holes, neutron stars and pulsars.
• Measures X-ray polarization from cosmic sources.
• Advances India's space astronomy research.
