❓ FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Astronomy FAQs

Quick answers to some of the most common questions about space and astronomy.

How old is the Universe?

The Universe is approximately 13.8 billion years old, according to measurements of the Cosmic Microwave Background and the expansion of space.

How many galaxies are there?

Astronomers estimate that the observable universe contains over 2 trillion galaxies.

What is the largest planet?

Jupiter is the largest planet in our Solar System, with a diameter of about 143,000 km.

Can black holes destroy galaxies?

No. Supermassive black holes are found at the centers of most galaxies, but they don't consume the entire galaxy.

Will the Milky Way collide with Andromeda?

Yes. Scientists predict the Milky Way and Andromeda will merge in about 4–5 billion years.

Which telescope is the most powerful?

The James Webb Space Telescope is currently the most powerful space telescope ever launched.
